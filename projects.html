Dance of Warriors is a 3D game currently being developed in Unity3D by myself, Cameron Markwell, Gauge Hartwell, and Aaron Koffel as part of our senior project at Oregon State University. This project seeks to explore the idea of a simulated dance in video games. Specifically, how to evoke this feeling of dancing between a human player and enemy AI. This game seeks to simulate this relationship through the use of rhythms, telegraphs, predictability in attack sequences, shifting who can be on the offense, and syncing actions with the music. This 3D Terrain Shader is an individual project created with GLSL. The project focuses on creating a 3D terrain while focusing on shader concepts such as displacement mapping, lighting, height mapping, and noise. It achieves this by creating a terrain generated with noise and increasing/decreasing the vertex locations depending on a height value obtained from said noise. Following this, the respective regions of the terrain can also be colored according to the height. With these concepts in mind it also applies the idea of terraces allowing the terrain to be adjusted into various terrace levels depending on a given input.
